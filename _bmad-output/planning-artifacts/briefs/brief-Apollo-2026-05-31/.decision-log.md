# Decision Log — Apollo Product Brief

## Session: 2026-05-31

### D1 — Psi existence treated as axiomatic
Psi effects are not under investigation in Apollo. The existing peer-reviewed literature (see Radin et al., 2026) is treated as sufficient to establish the phenomenon. A null result from Apollo indicts the arrangement, not the phenomenon.

### D2 — Double-blind as v1 default target framing
Western RV literature consistently insists on double-blind conditions as a validity control. This is accepted for v1 as the epistemologically conservative position. The question of whether front-loaded framing produces reliable results is explicitly deferred — not foreclosed.

### D3 — Email as session delivery and capture interface
Chosen for low friction, asynchrony, natural timestamping, and format agnosticism. Imposes minimal cognitive overhead on the asset at the moment of session. AI interpretation layer makes this workable at scale.

### D4 — ARV deferred from v1 scope
Asset is not versed in the ARV protocol. Architecture is designed to be compatible. Deferred, not excluded. Also noted: the target-question formulation approach used in v1 is structurally adjacent to ARV and may be characterised as a soft variant.

### D5 — Equal financial stake for the asset
Ethical position — no deontological alternative identified. Acknowledged as a potential confounding variable (motivation-psi interaction). System is designed to surface this if it manifests in the data.

### D6 — Success criteria to be empirically determined
No specific hit-rate threshold or p-value was established at brief stage. Working hypothesis: hit rate above chance at a confidence level sufficient to support positive expected value as a trading strategy. The system is designed to discover this number.

### D7 — Automated trade execution out of v1 scope
Signal informs human decision. Execution remains manual. No automated trading infrastructure in scope for first iteration.

### D8 — Secrecy hypothesis framed as structural, not conspiratorial
The absence of strong public claims of success is attributed to the zero-sum nature of market alpha: any genuine edge is rationally kept private. This is a falsifiable structural argument, not an unfalsifiable faith position. Included in brief on this basis.

### D9 — Non-financial targets included as protocol health measure
Small number of non-financial targets (e.g., factual historical questions) to be included in sessions. Results are not tradeable. Validation may not be achievable. Serve asset engagement and protocol variety. Epistemological status of unvalidatable sessions noted as a separate problem.

### D10 — Prediction horizon: hours to days preferred
System is agnostic on time horizon; asset capacity is treated as scarce. Hours-to-days preferred for operational practicality. Weeks possible. Longer horizons are ontologically neutral but logistically constrained.
